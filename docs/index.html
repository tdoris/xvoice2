XVoice2

Offline voice dictation for Linux.

Speak, and your words are typed into whatever app has focus, even a remote desktop. Hands-free with a spoken wake word. Private and local by default.

Download for Linux View on GitHub

A single AppImage. Download it, make it executable, and run it. No installer, no terminal required.

Why XVoice2

Hands-free

The mic is always on. Say your wake phrase to start, your sleep phrase to pause. No key to press.

Works over remote desktop

No global hotkey to be captured by an RDP or VNC client. Your local mic is transcribed and typed into the remote session.

Private and offline

Transcription runs entirely on your machine. Nothing is sent to the cloud unless you opt in.

Accurate, punctuated text

Uses NVIDIA Parakeet locally, with automatic punctuation and capitalization and no stray words on silence.

Lives in your tray

A simple tray app with a settings window: pick your engine, microphone, and wake phrases. No terminal needed.

Built for accessibility

Designed first for people who can't easily type. Fully controllable by voice.

Get started in three steps

  1. Download the XVoice2-x86_64.AppImage using the button above.
  2. Make it executable (right-click → Properties → Permissions → "Allow executing", or run chmod +x XVoice2-x86_64.AppImage), then double-click it.
  3. First launch downloads the speech model once (about 2.4 GB), then it is ready in your system tray. Say your wake word and start dictating.

Requirements: a 64-bit Linux desktop with a system tray. Text injection uses wtype (Wayland) or xdotool (X11), and the tray needs libxcb-cursor0 — install with sudo apt install wtype xdotool libxcb-cursor0 if needed.
